Why this conference

Built for a territory that worships in three languages.

The KwaZulu-Natal Free State Conference — headquartered in Pinetown and organised in 1994 — was the first multi-cultural Adventist conference in South Africa. Its congregations worship in English, Afrikaans and isiZulu, often within the same district.

That is exactly the problem SabbathCue is built for: a preacher who quotes Scripture faster than an operator can type, in more than one language, on an ordinary church projector.

The pilot lets media teams across the conference test verse detection in real services and share practical feedback that can inform the Afrikaans workflow and future language support.

In the app today

Bible, Ellen G. White and the hymnal — on one live screen.

Everything below ships in the current Windows release (v0.1.8). Nothing here is a promise — it is what your media desk gets on day one.

Detection

Verse detection, three ways

Direct references ("John 3:16"), quoted passages matched against Scripture, and reading mode that locks to the chapter being preached. A confidence slider tunes sensitivity to your room.

Ellen G. White

EGW writings, detected live

Browse, search and present from Education, Patriarchs and Prophets, Steps to Christ, The Desire of Ages and The Great Controversy. Spoken chapter, paragraph and page references are detected alongside Bible verses.

Hymnal

The SDA Hymnal built in

All 695 hymns, searchable by number, title or first line — pull a hymn's verses onto the live screen as cleanly as scripture.

Planning

Service planning

Build the whole service order ahead of time — scriptures, hymns and readings — then run it item by item on Sabbath morning with detection still listening in the background.

Display

Your screen, your look

A visual theme designer plus ten kinetic nature backgrounds — rain, snowfall, fireflies, aurora and more — animate gently behind the verse. Guided Projector Setup takes you live on HDMI in one tap.

Booth control

Built for the operator

Verse queue with drag-and-drop, keyboard-first quick navigation with autocomplete, voice switching between bundled translations, and Stream Deck / HTTP automation running on the SabbathCue computer.

Get Sabbath-ready

Four steps between now and your first live verse.

01

Install and sign in on the booth PC

Download the Windows installer and run it on the computer your media team already uses. Create your account and sign in once (first sign-in needs internet; one account covers two computers).

WINDOWS 10/11 · X64 · V0.1.8

02

Feed it the pulpit audio

Take a feed from the sound desk — or start with the PC microphone for a first test. Cleaner audio means more confident detections. Cloud transcription (Deepgram, Soniox, Speechmatics) is optional and uses your own API key.

ANY INPUT DEVICE · LEVEL METERING · CLOUD PROVIDER OPTIONS

03

Go live on the projector

Guided Projector Setup takes you live in one tap — it remembers last week's display, detects screens as you plug them in, and can identify each one on-screen. Pick a theme that matches your sanctuary look before service.

HDMI SECOND DISPLAY · GUIDED SETUP · THEME DESIGNER

04

Choose Manual or Auto mode

During the sermon, detections appear with a confidence score. Manual mode waits for the operator to preview and present each match; Auto mode can send high-confidence matches directly to the live output. In a loud room, raise the confidence threshold; chapter-only mentions hold as context until a verse is spoken.

MANUAL REVIEW · OPTIONAL AUTO MODE · CONFIDENCE THRESHOLD
