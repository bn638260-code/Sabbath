Honest expectations

It won't catch every verse. It will change how your service looks.

SabbathCue is AI, and AI isn't perfect — now and then it'll miss a reference or ask your operator for a quick confirm. But across a whole service it puts the right verse on screen faster and more often than someone scrambling alone. Your team ends up ahead: fewer missed references, less frantic searching, a cleaner stream. Better, not flawless — and a real step up from doing it all by hand.

In our own 106-case benchmark — spoken references, verbatim quotes, paraphrases, and ordinary announcements run through the live detection path — at the recommended 90% threshold SabbathCue caught 100% of clearly-spoken references, made the right call ~86% of the time overall, and the verse it put on screen was correct ~94% of the time. Every announcement and bit of small talk was correctly ignored. Loose paraphrases it's unsure about are surfaced for your operator rather than auto-fired. Measured on an authored test set, not live services.

Signal chain

From spoken word to live display in one calm workflow.

Four stages run while the sermon is live. Local Vosk can run offline with a verse-focused constrained grammar for Bible reference cues; Deepgram, Soniox, or Speechmatics runs only when you choose a cloud provider and add your own API key.

01 · INPUT

Listen

SabbathCue captures any audio input — the pulpit mic, your console's aux send, or a virtual device — with live level metering and voice activity detection.

CPAL · VAD · ANY DEVICE

02 · SPEECH

Transcribe

Speech becomes text as it is spoken. Run offline with the bundled Vosk model and verse-focused constrained grammar for English reference cues, or use Deepgram, recommended Soniox, or Speechmatics cloud transcription with your own API key and internet connection. Detect

Run it like a pro

The calm way to operate a live service.

A short field guide for the booth — the habits that keep the right verse on screen and keep mistakes off it.

①

Set up before the service

Choose your translation and background theme, then run a quick mic test so detection is warm before the first verse — not scrambling during the opening prayer.

②

Preview, then send live

Let detected verses land in Preview first, glance to confirm, then press Send Live. Keep a human between detection and the congregation's screen.

③

Tune the confidence slider

In a loud room, raise the semantic threshold to cut false hits; lower it if real verses get missed. Spoken references like “John 3:16” always surface regardless.

④

Queue the planned verses

Stack the sermon's known scriptures in the queue beforehand and fire them in order. Duplicates flash, so you never double-send the same verse.

⑤

Use reading mode for chapters

When the preacher reads a whole chapter, let reading mode follow along verse-by-verse — “next chapter”, “chapter five” — instead of chasing each line by hand.

⑥

Wait out chapter-only hits

“Turn to Revelation 20” shows as a chapter placeholder until the real verse is spoken. Wait for the specific verse before sending, and hit Clear to reset stale detections between passages.

Local-first & private

Your local setup stays under your control. Cloud transcription is optional.

  • Offline mode uses constrained grammar. Local Vosk is tuned for Bible reference cues and command phrases, not full-sermon free dictation. Use Deepgram, Soniox, or Speechmatics when you need broad cloud transcription quality.
  • Runs on your machine when local. Vosk transcription runs on the computer after the model is installed. Deepgram, Soniox, and Speechmatics require your own API key, internet connection, and provider account.
  • Your audio stays in the room with Vosk. Cloud transcription sends audio to the selected provider only when you explicitly choose Deepgram, Soniox, or Speechmatics.
  • Yours to control. Settings, history, and live-screen output live on your device; cloud speech is opt-in through your selected provider.
  • Bring your own translations. Licensed versions like NIV, ESV, NASB, NKJV, NLT, and AMP are supported when you hold the rights.
